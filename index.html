Sunline

The Mare of the World

As the 21st century dawned, a mare with speed, courage, and durability captured the hearts of racegoers in her native New Zealand, in Australia, and around the world.

Sunline emerged from her Auckland base to become one of the best race mares that New Zealand and Australia has ever seen. She was a cantankerous character who would bite any people who came too close, but she will long be remembered for her deeds on the track.

Sunline was always near the front in her races, inevitably pouring on the pressure with 600 meters to run. At her peak, few horses were able to go with her.

Bred by Susan Archer and Michael Martin, Sunline was leased to trainer Trevor McKee after he had success training Sunline’s family relative Interval, who he co-owned with Thayne Green and Helen Lusty. The lease terms gave McKee, Green, and Lusty a right of purchase for $NZ40,000 – an option they gladly took up shortly into her racing career.

Her list of accomplishments was spectacular. She won 13 group one races, and won five group one races on two occasions, including the W S Cox Plate and the Doncaster Handicap mile.

Sunline also performed well internationally, beating local champion Fairy King Prawn in the 2000 Hong Kong Mile.

In an astonishing career, Sunline was New Zealand Horse of the Year four times, and Australian Horse of the Year three times – both unmatched.

It was in Hong Kong that she was labeled “the mare of the world”. It was a term she well and truly earned. Few who followed racing at the time will forget her.

  • Born: September 29, 1995, at Pleasanton Farm, Cambridge, New Zealand.
  • Sire: Desert Sun. Dam: Songline.
  • Breeders: Susan Archer and Michael Martin, New Zealand.
  • Trainer: Trevor McKee, Takanini, Auckland, New Zealand.
  • Owners: Trevor McKee, Thayne Green, and Helen Lusty.
  • Race record: 48 starts, 32 wins, 9 seconds, 3 thirds.
  • Prizemoney earned: A$11,351,607.
  • Group One race victories:
    • W S Cox Plate (twice – 1999, 2000)
    • Doncaster Handicap (twice – 1999, 2002)
    • All-Aged Stakes (twice – 2000, 2002)
    • Coolmore Classic (twice – 2000, 2002)
    • Waikato Sprint (twice – 2001, 2002)
    • Flight Stakes – 1999
    • Manikato Stakes – 2000
    • Hong Kong Mile – 2000
  • New Zealand Horse of the Year: 1998-99, 1999-2000, 2000-01, 2001-02
  • Australian Horse of the Year: 1999-2000, 2000-01, 2001-02
